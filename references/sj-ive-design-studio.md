# Jony Ive Design Studio Reference

Use this reference for the `sj-ive-*` skills. It captures operating patterns from the Jony Ive and Apple Design Studio source notes without reproducing transcripts or book text.

## Core Principles

- Design begins with a story about how a person will meet and live with the thing. The story should be specific enough to shape features, materials, words, timing, and omissions.
- Care is perceived through small decisions. A user may not name the radius, weight, motion, sound, or sequence, but they can feel whether the maker was attentive.
- Technology should become more human as it becomes more powerful. The goal is not to expose machinery; it is to give people confidence, calm, agency, and pleasure.
- The first touch matters. Opening, wearing, launching, lifting, tapping, or hearing the product sets the trust contract for everything that follows.
- Taste improves through concrete prototypes. Many models, mocks, and side-by-side variants reveal tradeoffs that abstract debate hides.
- Materials and implementation are part of the idea. Form, finish, manufacturing method, engineering constraints, and supply reality can strengthen or betray the design story.
- Novelty must earn its place. Different is not enough; a new choice should be better in use, meaning, simplicity, trust, or emotional quality.
- Early ideas are fragile. They may need temporary protection from committees, metrics, premature feasibility judgment, or over-explanation until they have enough form to be inspected fairly.
- A product can move into the future without betraying its soul. Preserve the underlying promise, not every old shape, habit, or nostalgic signal.

## How This Connects To The Steve Jobs Skills

Steve Jobs skills in this pack emphasize focus, taste, story, product soul, direct artifact review, and the refusal to ship second-rate work. The Jony Ive layer makes those ideas more tactile: material, first touch, prototype volume, manufacturing, sensory care, and the emotional quality of technology.

Use `sj-product-*` skills when the main question is product direction or simplification. Use `sj-ive-*` skills when the question turns on form, feel, care, material, first encounter, prototype selection, or modernization without loss of soul.
