Source IDs support provenance and coverage checks without redistributing the underlying books or transcripts.
They indicate that a skill's operating claim was synthesized from a source, not that every sentence in the skill is a direct quotation or uncontested historical fact.

## Evidence Levels

- `direct`: the source explicitly presents the operating idea or a close paraphrase of it.
- `synthesized`: the operating idea combines multiple episodes or sources.
- `counterexample`: the source mainly grounds a warning, limitation, or failure mode.

The companion `sj-evidence-map.md` connects every skill to one claim ID, one or more source IDs, and an evidence level.
When historical precision matters, inspect the original source and state uncertainty.

## Copyright And Privacy Boundary

- Keep the original local source files outside this public repository.
- Store no full transcripts, private notes, secrets, or long excerpts in a skill or learning record.
- Prefer concise paraphrase and provenance IDs.
- Quote only when necessary, brief, and legally appropriate.
