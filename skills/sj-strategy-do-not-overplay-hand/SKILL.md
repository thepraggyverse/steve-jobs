# Do not overplay hand

## Core Move

Use leverage with restraint.

## Workflow

1. List actual leverage, alternatives, deadlines, dependencies, and information known by each side.
2. Separate durable leverage from temporary advantage, bluff, ego, and assumptions about the other party.
3. Define the minimum acceptable outcome, best alternative, and relationship value after the negotiation.
4. Choose a proportionate ask that preserves credibility and gives the other side a workable path to yes.
5. Precommit exit, pause, and de-escalation conditions before making irreversible threats.

## Guardrails

- Do not threaten actions you cannot or should not execute.
- Short-term extraction can destroy strategic trust.
- Power asymmetry does not remove legal or ethical duties.
