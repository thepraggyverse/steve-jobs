# Regret versus mistake

## Core Move

Separate reversible mistakes from life regrets.

## Workflow

1. State the decision, available information at the time, values involved, and realistic alternatives.
2. Separate process quality from outcome quality and identify hindsight knowledge unavailable then.
3. Classify the pain as a correctable mistake, accepted tradeoff, violated value, missed courage, or ordinary loss.
4. Choose repair, learning, recommitment, or release based on what remains changeable.
5. Write a future decision rule without pretending uncertainty can be eliminated.

## Guardrails

- Do not rewrite history using information gained later.
- Regret can signal values without proving the alternative would have succeeded.
- Self-forgiveness should accompany accountability, not replace it.
