# Confidant board

## Core Move

Use trusted outsiders to organize thought.

## Workflow

1. List the recurring decisions, blind spots, emotional pressures, and domains where independent counsel matters.
2. Select a small, diverse set of trusted people with judgment, discretion, and permission to disagree.
3. Assign each confidant a role, boundary, cadence, and type of question rather than creating a ceremonial board.
4. Create a compact briefing format and protect confidential or conflicted information.
5. Review whose advice changed decisions, where gaps remain, and whether any relationship has become dependent.

## Guardrails

- Do not outsource decisions or emotional regulation to the board.
- Disclose conflicts and protect third-party confidentiality.
- Fame and agreement are poor selection criteria.
