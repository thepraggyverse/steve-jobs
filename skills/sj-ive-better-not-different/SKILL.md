# Better Not Different

## Core Move

Force every novel choice to prove a real improvement in use, meaning, trust, simplicity, or emotional quality.

## Workflow

1. Name the different or novel choice.
2. Identify what improvement it claims to create.
3. Compare that claim against user behavior and product story.
4. Find the cost paid for being different.
5. Decide whether to keep, refine, or remove the novelty.

## Output

Return a compact result with:

- Novel choice
- Claimed improvement
- Evidence for better
- Cost of different
- Decision

## Guardrails

- Do not reward novelty because it photographs well.
- Do not reject novelty simply because it is unfamiliar.
- Anchor the decision in use and meaning.
- Do not use long quotations from the source material.

## Example Prompt

Use $sj-ive-better-not-different to test whether this unusual design choice is actually better.
