# Historical analogy

## Core Move

Use history to make the future understandable.

## Workflow

1. Name the unfamiliar product change and the audience misconception blocking understanding.
2. Find a familiar historical transition with the same causal structure, actors, and constraint shift.
3. Map the old roles to the new ones and mark where the analogy stops matching.
4. Use the analogy to explain one consequence, not to prove market size or inevitability.
5. Rewrite the positioning in plain language and test whether it remains true without the analogy.

## Output

Return a compact result with:

- New change
- Historical parallel
- Structural mapping
- Breakpoints
- Plain-language positioning

## Guardrails

- Reject analogies based only on surface resemblance.
- Do not treat historical recurrence as evidence of inevitable success.
- State the analogy's limit before it can mislead the audience.
