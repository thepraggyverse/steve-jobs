# Build for yourself

## Core Move

Build what the team deeply wants and understands.

## Workflow

1. Describe the founder or team's own recurring problem, current workaround, and cost of leaving it unsolved.
2. Test whether the team is representative of a larger reachable segment or an unusual edge case.
3. Use internal daily use to expose quality, speed, and workflow failures while gathering external counterevidence.
4. Separate strong product intuition from preferences that do not generalize or support a business.
5. Define the external validation required before turning personal conviction into roadmap priority.

## Output

Return a compact result with:

- Internal problem
- Representativeness test
- Dogfood evidence
- Non-generalizable preferences
- External validation gate

## Guardrails

- Being the customer improves access to pain, not certainty about the market.
- Do not dismiss accessibility or unfamiliar-user needs because insiders cope.
- Internal enthusiasm cannot substitute for willingness to adopt or pay.
