# Ed Catmull patience

## Core Move

Use facts, trust, and time to change strong minds.

## Workflow

1. Name the important disagreement, the other person's model, and the cost of forcing immediate agreement.
2. Identify shared goals and the evidence each side would recognize as meaningful.
3. Replace repeated persuasion with a sequence of questions, prototypes, and low-risk exposures.
4. Give the idea enough time to become understood while setting a decision deadline tied to real constraints.
5. Track movement in assumptions and decide whether to continue, adapt, or stop the persuasion effort.

## Guardrails

- Patience is not indefinite avoidance.
- Do not manipulate, exhaust, or socially isolate the dissenter.
- Allow evidence to change your own position too.
