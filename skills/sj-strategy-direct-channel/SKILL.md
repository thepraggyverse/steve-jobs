# Direct channel

## Core Move

Build a direct relationship with customers.

## Workflow

1. Map every intermediary between product team and customer, including data, incentives, delays, and distorted signals.
2. Choose the customer decisions where direct education, purchase, support, or feedback would improve the experience.
3. Design a direct touchpoint that provides unique value rather than merely shifting acquisition cost.
4. Define ownership, economics, service levels, consent, and how partner conflicts will be handled.
5. Pilot the loop and compare learning speed, customer quality, margin, and retention with existing channels.

## Guardrails

- Direct does not automatically mean cheaper, trusted, or convenient.
- Preserve privacy, consent, and viable partner relationships.
- Do not own a channel the organization cannot support reliably.
