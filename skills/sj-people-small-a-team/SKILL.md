# Small A-team

## Core Move

Prefer a small excellent team over a large average one.

## Workflow

1. Define the smallest complete outcome and the deadline or learning target.
2. List only the capabilities and decisions required to deliver it end to end.
3. Select a compact team of high-agency people with complementary skills and clear availability.
4. Remove observers, duplicate roles, and approval hops while assigning one owner per interface.
5. Set a direct communication cadence and revisit team size only when a proven capability gap appears.

## Guardrails

- Small does not mean understaffed or permanently overloaded.
- Do not exclude essential safety, legal, accessibility, or operational expertise.
- Add people for demonstrated work, not political representation.
